## Explanation

Indefinite articles correspond to English "a/an" (singular) and "some" (plural):

- **un** — masculine singular: *un livre* (a book)
- **une** — feminine singular: *une table* (a table)
- **des** — plural (all genders): *des enfants* (some children / children)

After negation, **un/une/des** all become **de** (or **d'** before a vowel):
- *J'ai un chien.* → *Je n'ai pas de chien.* (I don't have a dog.)

## Examples

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| un homme | a man |
| une femme | a woman |
| des livres | (some) books |
| Je n'ai pas d'ami. | I have no friend. |

## Pitfall

English often omits "some" (*I have books*), but French always uses *des* (*J'ai des livres*). Don't skip the article.
