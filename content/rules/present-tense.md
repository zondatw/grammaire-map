## Explanation

Most French verbs end in **-er** (e.g., *parler*, *manger*, *travailler*). To conjugate them in the present tense, drop the **-er** and add these endings:

| Person | Ending | Example (parler) |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| je | -e | parle |
| tu | -es | parles |
| il/elle | -e | parle |
| nous | -ons | parlons |
| vous | -ez | parlez |
| ils/elles | -ent | parlent |

Note: je, tu, il/elle, and ils/elles share the same *sound* (the ending is silent) — this is a key feature of French pronunciation.

## Examples

- *Je parle français.* — I speak French.
- *Tu travailles beaucoup.* — You work a lot.
- *Nous mangeons ensemble.* — We eat together.
- *Ils écoutent de la musique.* — They listen to music.

## Pitfall

The -ent ending (ils/elles) is **always silent**. *Ils parlent* is pronounced the same as *il parle*. Context and subject pronouns carry the grammatical weight in spoken French.
