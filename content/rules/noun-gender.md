## Explanation

Every French noun is either **masculine** or **feminine** — there is no neuter. Gender must be memorized, but several patterns help predict it.

**Common masculine endings:** -age, -ment, -eau, -isme, -eur (agent nouns)
**Common feminine endings:** -tion, -sion, -ité, -ette, -ance, -ence, -ure

## Examples

| Masculine | Feminine |
|-----------|----------|
| le fromage (cheese) | la nation |
| le gouvernement | la liberté |
| le château | la voiture (car) |
| le tourisme | la chance |

## Pitfall

Patterns are guides, not rules. *le problème* ends in -e but is masculine; *la main* (hand) ends in -in but is feminine. Always confirm with a dictionary when unsure, and learn articles with nouns from day one.
