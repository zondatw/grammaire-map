## Explanation

French adjectives **agree in gender and number** with the noun they modify.

**Rules:**
- Masculine singular: base form (e.g., *grand*)
- Feminine singular: add **-e** (e.g., *grande*) — unless already ends in -e
- Masculine plural: add **-s** (e.g., *grands*)
- Feminine plural: add **-es** (e.g., *grandes*)

Most adjectives follow this pattern. Some have irregular feminine forms.

## Examples

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|-|-----------|----------|
| Singular | un homme grand | une femme grande |
| Plural | des hommes grands | des femmes grandes |

Irregular example:
- *beau* (beautiful, masc.) → *belle* (fem.)
- *vieux* (old, masc.) → *vieille* (fem.)
- *bon* (good, masc.) → *bonne* (fem.)

## Pitfall

In French, most adjectives come **after** the noun (unlike English): *une voiture rouge* (a red car), not *une rouge voiture*. A small group of common adjectives (BAGS: Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size) precede the noun: *un grand homme*, *une belle femme*, *un bon repas*.
