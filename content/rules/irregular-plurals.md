## Explanation

Most French nouns form the plural by adding **-s** (which is silent). But several patterns are irregular:

**-al → -aux**
- *un cheval* → *des chevaux* (horse → horses)
- *un journal* → *des journaux* (newspaper)

**-eau → -eaux** (already ends in silent -s, add x)
- *un gâteau* → *des gâteaux* (cake → cakes)
- *un chapeau* → *des chapeaux* (hat)

**-eu → -eux**
- *un jeu* → *des jeux* (game → games)

**Already ends in -s, -x, -z:** no change
- *un bras* → *des bras* (arm), *une voix* → *des voix* (voice)

## Examples

| Singular | Plural |
|----------|--------|
| un animal | des animaux |
| un bateau | des bateaux |
| un feu | des feux |
| un bras | des bras |

## Pitfall

*travail* → *travaux* (work), not *travails*. Learn irregular plurals with the noun.
