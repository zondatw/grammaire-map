## Explanation

The subjunctive is a mood (not a tense) used in subordinate clauses after expressions of **doubt, emotion, necessity, or desire**.

**Common triggers:**
- *il faut que* (it is necessary that)
- *je veux que* (I want that)
- *je suis content(e) que* (I am happy that)
- *bien que* (although)

**Formation:** Take the *ils/elles* present stem + subjunctive endings:

| Person | Ending |
|--------|--------|
| je | -e |
| tu | -es |
| il/elle | -e |
| nous | -ions |
| vous | -iez |
| ils/elles | -ent |

Example — *parler* (ils parlent → stem: parl-):
*que je parle, que tu parles, qu'il parle…*

**Irregular stems:** *être (soi-/som-)*, *avoir (ai-/ay-)*, *aller (aill-/all-)*, *faire (fass-/fas-)*

## Examples

- *Il faut que tu parles plus fort.* (You need to speak louder.)
- *Je suis content qu'elle soit là.* (I'm glad she's here.)

## Pitfall

The subjunctive always requires **que** and a different subject. *Je veux partir* (same subject, use infinitive) vs *Je veux qu'il parte* (different subject, use subjunctive).
