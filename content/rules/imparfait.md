## Explanation

The imparfait describes:
1. **Ongoing/habitual past actions** — what used to happen, or what was happening
2. **Background state** in a past narrative

**Formation:** Take the *nous* present stem + imperfect endings:

| Person | Ending |
|--------|--------|
| je | -ais |
| tu | -ais |
| il/elle | -ait |
| nous | -ions |
| vous | -iez |
| ils/elles | -aient |

Example — *parler* (nous parlons → stem: parl-):
- *je parlais, tu parlais, il parlait, nous parlions…*

**Only être is irregular:** stem = *ét-* (*j'étais, tu étais…*)

## Examples

- *Quand j'étais enfant, je jouais au foot.* (When I was a child, I played football.)
- *Il pleuvait quand je suis sorti.* (It was raining when I went out.) ← background state

## Pitfall

**Passé composé vs imparfait:** Use passé composé for completed, punctual events; use imparfait for ongoing background. *J'ai mangé* (I ate — a completed event). *Je mangeais* (I was eating / I used to eat).
