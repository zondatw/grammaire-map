## Explanation

Partitive articles express an unspecified quantity of something uncountable — corresponding roughly to English "some" or no article at all:

- **du** — masculine singular: *du pain* (some bread)
- **de la** — feminine singular: *de la musique* (some music)
- **de l'** — before vowel/silent h: *de l'eau* (some water)
- **des** — plural (already covered in indefinite articles)

After negation, all become **de/d'**:
- *Je mange du pain.* → *Je ne mange pas de pain.*

## Examples

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| Je veux du café. | I want (some) coffee. |
| Elle mange de la soupe. | She eats (some) soup. |
| Nous buvons de l'eau. | We drink (some) water. |

## Pitfall

English often omits "some" (*I want coffee*), but French requires the partitive: *Je veux du café*. Omitting it sounds foreign. After negation, *du/de la* → *de*: *Je ne veux pas de café*.
