## Explanation

French possessive adjectives agree with the **noun they modify** (not the owner):

| Owner | Masc. singular | Fem. singular | Plural |
|-------|---------------|---------------|--------|
| my | mon | ma | mes |
| your (tu) | ton | ta | tes |
| his/her/its | son | sa | ses |
| our | notre | notre | nos |
| your (vous) | votre | votre | vos |
| their | leur | leur | leurs |

Before a feminine word starting with a vowel: use **mon/ton/son** (not ma/ta/sa):
- *mon amie* (my friend, feminine)

## Examples

- *mon livre* (my book) — livre is masculine
- *ma maison* (my house) — maison is feminine
- *ses enfants* (his/her children) — plural

## Pitfall

*Son/sa/ses* can mean his, her, or its — context tells you which. *Sa voiture* could be his car or her car. French doesn't distinguish like English does.
