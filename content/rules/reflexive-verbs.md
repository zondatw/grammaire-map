## Explanation

Reflexive verbs use a reflexive pronoun that refers back to the subject. They indicate the subject is doing something to/for themselves.

**Reflexive pronouns:** me, te, se, nous, vous, se

Example — *se laver* (to wash oneself):

| Person | Conjugation |
|--------|-------------|
| je | me lave |
| tu | te laves |
| il/elle | se lave |
| nous | nous lavons |
| vous | vous lavez |
| ils/elles | se lavent |

Before vowels: *me → m'*, *te → t'*, *se → s'*

## Examples

- *Je me lève à 7h.* — I get up at 7.
- *Elle s'appelle Marie.* — Her name is Marie. (lit. She calls herself Marie.)
- *Nous nous couchons tard.* — We go to bed late.

## Pitfall

Many common daily-routine verbs are reflexive in French: *se réveiller* (to wake up), *se lever* (to get up), *s'habiller* (to get dressed), *se coucher* (to go to bed). Memorize these as reflexive from the start.
