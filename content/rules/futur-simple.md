## Explanation

The futur simple expresses future events. It's formed by adding endings directly to the **infinitive** (for -er and -ir verbs) or to the **infinitive minus final -e** (for -re verbs):

| Person | Ending |
|--------|--------|
| je | -ai |
| tu | -as |
| il/elle | -a |
| nous | -ons |
| vous | -ez |
| ils/elles | -ont |

- *parler → je parlerai, tu parleras…*
- *finir → je finirai…*
- *vendre → je vendrai…* (drop -e)

**Common irregular stems:**
- *être → ser-* (*je serai*)
- *avoir → aur-* (*j'aurai*)
- *aller → ir-* (*j'irai*)
- *faire → fer-* (*je ferai*)
- *pouvoir → pourr-* (*je pourrai*)

## Examples

- *Demain, je travaillerai toute la journée.* (Tomorrow I will work all day.)
- *Quand tu auras le temps, appelle-moi.* (When you have time, call me.)

## Pitfall

In subordinate clauses after *quand, lorsque, dès que, aussitôt que*, French uses the futur simple — where English uses the present: *Quand il viendra...* (When he comes / will come...).
