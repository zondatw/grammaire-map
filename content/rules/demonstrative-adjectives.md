## Explanation

Demonstrative adjectives correspond to English "this/that/these/those":

- **ce** — masculine singular: *ce livre* (this/that book)
- **cet** — masculine singular before vowel/silent h: *cet homme* (this man)
- **cette** — feminine singular: *cette maison* (this/that house)
- **ces** — plural (all genders): *ces enfants* (these/those children)

French does not distinguish "this" vs "that" or "these" vs "those" with the adjective alone. To add that nuance, append **-ci** (here) or **-là** (there) to the noun:
- *ce livre-ci* (this book here) / *ce livre-là* (that book there)

## Examples

- *ce garçon* — this/that boy
- *cet arbre* — this/that tree (arbre starts with a vowel)
- *cette femme* — this/that woman
- *ces voitures* — these/those cars

## Pitfall

Don't use *ce* before a vowel — use *cet*: *cet avion* (this plane), not *ce avion*.
