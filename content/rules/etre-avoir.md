## Explanation

**Être** (to be) and **avoir** (to have) are the two most important French verbs. They are both irregular and serve as auxiliary verbs in compound tenses.

### Être — présent
| Person | Form |
|--------|------|
| je | suis |
| tu | es |
| il/elle | est |
| nous | sommes |
| vous | êtes |
| ils/elles | sont |

### Avoir — présent
| Person | Form |
|--------|------|
| je | ai |
| tu | as |
| il/elle | a |
| nous | avons |
| vous | avez |
| ils/elles | ont |

## Examples

- *Je suis étudiant.* — I am a student.
- *Elle a un chat.* — She has a cat.
- *Nous sommes en France.* — We are in France.
- *Vous avez faim?* — Are you hungry? (lit. Do you have hunger?)

## Pitfall

French uses *avoir* for hunger, thirst, age, and fear — where English uses "to be": *J'ai faim* (I am hungry), *J'ai vingt ans* (I am twenty years old). Don't translate "I am hungry" as *Je suis faim*.
