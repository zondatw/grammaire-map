## Explanation

Relative pronouns introduce subordinate clauses that describe a noun.

- **qui** — subject of the relative clause (who, which, that)
  - *L'homme qui parle est mon père.* (The man who is speaking is my father.)

- **que/qu'** — direct object of the relative clause (whom, which, that)
  - *Le film que j'ai vu est excellent.* (The film that I saw is excellent.)

- **dont** — replaces *de + noun* (whose, of which, about which)
  - *Le livre dont je parle est célèbre.* (The book I'm talking about is famous.)

- **où** — place or time (where, when)
  - *La ville où j'habite est belle.* (The city where I live is beautiful.)

## Pitfall

**qui vs que:** *qui* is the subject (it has no following noun phrase), *que* is the object (followed by subject + verb). In *L'homme qui parle*, *qui* is the subject doing the speaking. In *L'homme que je vois*, *que* is the object — *je* is the subject.
