## Explanation

French negation uses a two-part structure that **wraps the verb**:

**ne** + verb + **pas**

- *Je parle.* → *Je ne parle pas.* (I don't speak.)
- *Elle mange.* → *Elle ne mange pas.* (She doesn't eat.)

Before a vowel or silent h, **ne** contracts to **n'**:
- *Il aime.* → *Il n'aime pas.*

In spoken French, **ne** is often dropped: *Je parle pas.* (informal)

## Examples

| Affirmative | Negative |
|-------------|----------|
| Je parle français. | Je ne parle pas français. |
| Tu as faim. | Tu n'as pas faim. |
| Nous mangeons. | Nous ne mangeons pas. |

## Pitfall

Don't forget the **ne** — even though it's dropped in speech, it's required in writing. The pair **ne…pas** must surround the conjugated verb, not the infinitive.
