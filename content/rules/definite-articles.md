## Explanation

French has three definite articles that correspond to English "the":

- **le** — masculine singular: *le chien* (the dog)
- **la** — feminine singular: *la maison* (the house)
- **les** — plural (all genders): *les enfants* (the children)

Before a vowel or silent **h**, both *le* and *la* contract to **l'**:
- *l'ami* (the friend, masculine), *l'école* (the school, feminine)

## Examples

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| le livre | the book |
| la table | the table |
| les chats | the cats |
| l'eau | the water |

## Pitfall

Don't confuse **le/la** with gender — you must know the noun's gender to pick the right article. When in doubt, learn the article with the noun as a unit: not just *chien* but *le chien*.
