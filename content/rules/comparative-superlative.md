## Explanation

**Comparative** (more/less/as … than):
- *plus + adjective + que* — more than: *plus grand que* (taller than)
- *moins + adjective + que* — less than: *moins grand que* (shorter than)
- *aussi + adjective + que* — as … as: *aussi grand que* (as tall as)

**Superlative** (the most/least):
- *le/la/les + plus + adjective* — the most: *le plus grand* (the tallest)
- *le/la/les + moins + adjective* — the least: *le moins grand*

The adjective still agrees in gender/number with the noun.

**Irregular forms:**
- *bon → meilleur* (better), *le meilleur* (the best)
- *mauvais → pire* (worse), *le pire* (the worst)

## Examples

- *Paris est plus grand que Lyon.* (Paris is bigger than Lyon.)
- *C'est le meilleur film de l'année.* (It's the best film of the year.)
- *Elle est moins fatiguée que lui.* (She is less tired than him.)

## Pitfall

Don't say *plus bon* — the correct form is *meilleur*. Similarly, *plus mauvais* exists but *pire* is preferred.
